# Acceptance-criteria additions — PROPOSAL (issue #27 audit)

> **Status: PROPOSAL — not yet adopted.** This document is the synthesized output of
> the issue #27 *adversarial audit*, which read the implementation against the spec
> and surfaced **implicit requirements** the code already honors (or contradicts) but
> that `acceptance.html`, `COVERAGE.md`, and the schemas do not yet pin. Each row below
> proposes a criterion that would freeze one such behavior. Nothing here is wired into a
> suite; adopting a row means (a) deciding the contract where the audit flagged a *choice
> the spec never makes*, (b) writing the assertion under the recommended method, and
> (c) folding it into `COVERAGE.md` with a real requirement id.

It mirrors the spirit of `COVERAGE.md`: every proposed criterion maps to the **requirement**
it pins, its **gap status** (`partial` = some coverage exists, the assertion sharpens or
extends it; `unspecified` = no spec/AC language at all, the contract must first be *decided*),
the **recommended AC method** (the five of `acceptance.html` — **BDD · PROP · GOLD · JUDGE ·
MANUAL**), the **concrete assertion** it would make, and the **target context doc** plus a
**suggested requirement id** to host it.

Rows are grouped by **primary AC method**, since the method dictates where the proof lives
and who owns it. A criterion needing two methods is filed under its load-bearing one and
notes the secondary. Obvious duplicates in the audit feed (the same behavior raised from
the schema lens and the feature lens, e.g. backup change-detection-by-hash, checkpoint-
before-copy, quick_check semantics, the one-open index, settings read-strictness, the
monotonic clamp, switch atomicity, gap-threshold, subtract reversibility, the CSV
contract) are merged into a single row, citing both sources.

The audit's running insight: **anything that touches a billable number or the on-disk
record is owned by core and must be pinned by PROP/GOLD — JUDGE is never the sole gate.**
